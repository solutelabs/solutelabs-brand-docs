# ICP & Messaging

## Who We Serve Best

Our best clients share one defining characteristic above all others:

**They understand their own business deeply.**

They have domain expertise, existing customers, a proven model — and they know what outcome they're chasing. What they need is a technology partner who can match their business understanding with engineering capability and help them figure out what to build and how.

### The Pattern Across Client Types

| Client Type | What They Had | What We Added |
|---|---|---|
| Manufacturing / operations businesses | Deep domain expertise, proven processes | Technology layer on top of proven operations |
| B2B SaaS with PMF | Validated product, paying customers | Alternate growth pipelines, AI feature development |
| Large enterprises (e.g. Synergy Marine Group) | Scale, internal expertise, a CTO navigating complexity | End-to-end internal tooling and digital transformation |
| Professional services businesses | Strong client relationships, service delivery knowledge | Automation, AI tools, efficiency without disruption |

**The insight:** We are not generalists who take any project. We complement people who already know what they're doing. We are the technology side of a partnership where the client brings domain mastery.

## Primary Buyer Personas

### Founders / CEOs (Startups & Small Companies)

The majority of our work. Two sub-types:

**Technical founders (Seed — Series B)** — Building their first real product or rebuilding one that's hit a ceiling. They know enough about engineering to ask good questions. They want to move fast without creating technical debt. They will read the detail — show them the architecture, the tools, the trade-offs.

**Non-technical founders with a business problem (not a spec)** — They know their outcome. They don't know the solution. They need someone to help them think through what to build before building it. This is where our judgment-first approach wins most decisively.

**What they have in common:** They're the decision-maker. Conversations are direct, move fast, and the relationship is personal.

### CTOs / VP Engineering / CPOs (Growth-Stage SaaS & Enterprise)

This is who we work with at larger companies — Amagi, Roche, Synergy Marine. They're evaluating an external partner to augment or lead a specific workstream. They're skeptical of AI hype. They want to see how we actually build — the specs, the orchestration, the verification layer. Prove capability first, sell second.

**CPOs / Heads of Product** lean less technical — they care about timelines, costs, and outcomes. They want to hear "MVP in weeks, not quarters" and see the case studies to back it up.

**CTOs / VP Engineering** want the technical depth. Show them the architecture, the harness, the verification layer. They'll judge us on process, not promises.

**What they have in common:** They're navigating internal stakeholders. Our proposals need to help them sell us internally — with proof points, clear scope, and risk mitigation.

## Best-Fit Signal Set

A prospect is highly likely to be a strong fit if:

- They have an existing, operating business — not a pre-revenue idea
- They can describe the business problem they're trying to solve (not just a feature list)
- They're open to being challenged on their approach
- They've had a bad experience with a dev shop that just "did what they were told"
- They're actively using or evaluating AI tools (OpenAI, Anthropic, ElevenLabs, LangChain, etc.)
- They're in B2B SaaS, operations-heavy industries, professional services, or enterprise with a digital transformation mandate

## Worst-Fit Signal Set

Disqualify early (and do it kindly — as we have since 2014) if:

- They have a rough idea and want us to validate it for them (the idea needs refinement before engineering)
- They have a rigid spec and want it executed without discussion
- They're looking for the cheapest option
- They have no existing customers or revenue to fund a proper build
- They want us to build and then hand over — no ongoing relationship

## Messaging by Buyer Stage

### Stage 1 — Unaware / Not Actively Looking

**Goal:** Create awareness and curiosity. Plant the idea that their current approach has a ceiling.

**Message:** "Most companies are building AI features the hard way — with the wrong team or the wrong process. Here's what the difference looks like."

**Channels:** LinkedIn (Karan's personal brand), blog, newsletter

### Stage 2 — Problem Aware / Exploring Options

**Goal:** Position SoluteLabs as the right category of solution for their specific problem.

**Message:** "If you have a proven business and need to build the technology layer that scales it — not just add features, but build systems that compound — that's what we do."

**Channels:** Blog, case studies, referral conversations, targeted outreach

### Stage 3 — Solution Aware / Evaluating Partners

**Goal:** Win the credibility and trust evaluation. Differentiate on judgment and process, not just price or speed.

**Message:** "Here's exactly how we work. Here's what the Agentic Harness means in practice. Here's a client who can tell you what it was like."

**Channels:** Website, proposals, case studies, intro calls, client references

### Stage 4 — Decision Ready

**Goal:** Remove risk. Make it easy to say yes.

**Message:** "Start with one scoped workstream. You'll know early whether this is the right partnership."

**Channels:** Proposals, final conversations, pilot project framing
