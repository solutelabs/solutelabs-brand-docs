# Karan's Voice

Karan's public persona is "The Builder-CEO Who Rebuilt on AI" — at the intersection of agency leadership, AI-native operations, and startup ecosystem wisdom. 16+ years in tech (since 2010), founded SoluteLabs in 2014.

---

## Core Narrative

> "I run a product studio that punches way above its weight — because we rebuilt everything around AI agents. Here's what I'm learning along the way."

This is a journey, not a destination. Technical buyers respect honesty about what's working and what isn't more than they respect polished success stories. The throughline: you don't need a large team, you need a strong one.

## Content Pillars

1. **AI-Native Agency Operations (40%)** — How SoluteLabs uses AI agents internally. What's working, what failed, specific tools and workflows, real metrics. This is Karan's most unique angle.
2. **AI Product Development Insights (30%)** — Technical-enough content about building AI products. Architectural decisions, trade-offs, implementation lessons. Not too deep for founder/CEO buyers.
3. **Startup & Scaling Wisdom (20%)** — A decade-plus of building products for startups. Accumulated wisdom about MVPs, fundraising, product-market fit, scaling.
4. **Personal & Team Culture (10%)** — Human content. Running a team, hiring, co-founder dynamics. Use sparingly but consistently.

### Writing patterns across all posts

- **One sentence per line.** Heavy use of line breaks. Never walls of text.
- **Story-first.** Opens with a specific moment, person, or action — not a thesis statement.
- **Nuanced takes.** Doesn't take the obvious side. Finds the middle, or reframes the question entirely. ("So who's right? Both.")
- **Names names.** Specific tools (Claude Code, GA4, DataforSEO), specific clients (Roche, Amagi, FSI), specific places (Bali, Brisbane, Ahmedabad).
- **Self-aware honesty.** "It's... fine." / "I'm not saying this to flex." / "I've actually paid a few over the years!"
- **Parallel structure with em dashes.** "Vibe coding works — for prototypes. Spec-driven agentic engineering works — for production."
- **Rhetorical questions.** "So who's right?" / "What do you think?"
- **Short closers.** Ends with a verb, a question, or a one-line punch — never a summary paragraph.
- **Range.** Goes from deeply technical to deeply personal. Doesn't stay in one lane.
- **Emoji.** Very sparingly, only in personal/reflective posts. Never in technical content.
- **Arrows (→)** for scannable lists when listing requirements or steps.

## What Karan Should NOT Sound Like

- Corporate: "We're thrilled to announce..."
- Generic AI commentary without personal take
- ChatGPT-default voice without heavy editing
- Sales pitch disguised as thought leadership
- Argumentative in comments

---

## Engagement Style

- **Comments:** Responds personally. Down to earth, conversational, generous with insight. Treats comments as conversations, not performances.
- **Debates:** Engages thoughtfully but doesn't argue. States his position clearly, acknowledges the other side, moves on.
- **DMs:** Open to them. Prefers directness — "happy to chat" is genuine, not a brush-off.
- **Tone in replies:** Same as posts — direct, warm, no corporate veneer.

## Publishing Rules

- Karan's social posts require his personal review and voice editing before publishing, regardless of who or what drafted them.
- Ghostwriting is fine. Publishing without his approval is not.
